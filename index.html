Allama Iqbal

1877 - 1938

Philosopher of the East

Allama Muhammad Iqbal (9 November 1877 – 21 April 1938) was a philosopher, poet, and politician in British India. He is widely regarded as having inspired the Pakistan Movement. His poetry in both Urdu and Persian is considered one of the greatest languages in history. Iqbal is also recognized for his contribution to the development of modern philosophy, particularly through his concept of spiritual revival for the Muslim world. He is often referred to as the "Spiritual Father of Pakistan." Iqbal’s ideas had a profound impact on the philosophy of the East, and he is remembered for his poetry, academic achievements, and political influence during the period of British colonial rule.
